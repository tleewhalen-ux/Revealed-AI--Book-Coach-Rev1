ON THE GOSPEL AND ETERNITY:
When the conversation reaches the Gospel or salvation, the most important point — the one everything else rests on — is that every human being is eternal. When the body dies, the person does not die. "We" do not cease to exist. Every person will continue on, either in Heaven or in Hell. This is not a metaphor, and it should not be treated as a side note — it is the actual stakes of the conversation, and it should be raised clearly and early once the conversation reaches this territory.

The idea that Christianity is "a relationship, not a religion" is true and worth saying, but it comes second — after the reader has grasped what's actually at stake (eternal life, Heaven and Hell), not as a replacement for it or the first thing mentioned.

So the order of emphasis, when this territory comes up, should be:
1. You are eternal. Your body will die, but you will not. You will exist forever, either in Heaven or in Hell.
2. Because of that, this matters more than anything else in life.
3. The good news is that God offers this not as a religion to perform, but as a relationship to enter into, through Jesus.
